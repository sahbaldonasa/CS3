Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift's debut studio album. Big Machine Records published it on October 24, 2006, in the United States and Canada, and on March 18, 2008, globally.

Fearless

Fearless is Taylor Swift's second studio album. Big Machine Records published it in the United States and Canada on November 11, 2008, and a worldwide version was released on March 9, 2009.

Speak Now

Speak Now is Taylor Swift's third studio album, released on October 25, 2010, via Big Machine Records. Swift created the album entirely on her own in less than two years, while promoting her second studio album, Fearless.

Red

Taylor Swift's fourth studio album, Red, is her fourth studio album. Big Machine Records released it on October 22, 2012.

1989

Taylor Swift's fifth studio album, 1989, is her fifth studio album. Big Machine Records released it on October 27, 2014.

Reputation

Taylor Swift's sixth studio album, Reputation, is her sixth studio album. It was her final album for Big Machine Records, and it was released on November 10, 2017.

Lover

Taylor Swift's seventh studio album, Lover, is her seventh studio album. It was published by Republic Records on August 23, 2019, and is her first album release following her departure from Big Machine Records, which culminated in a widely publicized disagreement.

Folklore

Taylor Swift's eighth studio album, Folklore (stylized in all lowercase), is her eighth studio album. It was a surprise album, released by Republic Records on July 24, 2020. Swift canceled the concert tour for her seventh studio album Lover when the COVID-19 pandemic broke out in early 2020.

Evermore

Taylor Swift's ninth studio album, Evermore (stylized in all lowercase), is her ninth studio album. It was a surprise album released on December 11, 2020, by Republic Records, just five months after her eighth studio album, Folklore.

Midnights

Midnights is Taylor Swift's tenth studio album, which was released on October 21, 2022, by Republic Records. Swift's first body of new music since her 2020 alternative folk albums, Folklore and Evermore, was unveiled during the 2022 MTV Video Music Awards.
